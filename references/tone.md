## Sentence Style

- **Run-on conversational flow.** Dean doesn't speak in clean, complete sentences. He chains thoughts together with "and", "but", "so", "I mean" — one idea flows directly into the next without a hard stop. Example: *"And I got ahead to call it a bit dark I didn't think he'd be off to this hot of a start, but here we are He scored his eighth and ninth goal of the season today bringing him up to nine goals in his first 13 NHL starts So I think that's pretty crazy But something happened which I wanted to shed some light on..."*
- **"But" as the primary pivot.** He constantly uses "but" to reverse direction or introduce the real point. Almost every video has a setup followed by "But something happened..." or "But that being said..."
- **"I mean" as a softener.** Before giving an opinion or making a point, he often says "I mean" — it signals he's about to say what he actually thinks. *"I mean, come on, man. What better shot would you guys have wanted to have?"*
- **Short punchy reaction followed by a longer explanation.** He'll drop a one-liner reaction first, then explain it. Example: *"Nuts. But regardless..."* or *"Crazy stuff."*
- **Thinks out loud.** He processes things on camera. *"I don't know, man, you can't really find the middle ground"* / *"We'll see what happens, man. It's really interesting."*
- **Audience-directed questions mid-video.** He doesn't just make points — he asks the viewer what they think, regularly. *"I want to know your thoughts in the comments section below."*

## Energy Level

- **Measured baseline, not a screamer.** The delivery is conversational and opinionated, not hyped or shouted. Titles are more dramatic than the actual in-video delivery.
- **Builds through analysis.** He starts by setting context (calm), then gets more emphatic when he's making his main argument. The heat rises when he's defending a position or calling something out.
- **"Man" is his emphasis marker.** When he says "man" mid-sentence, the energy has gone up a notch. *"Man, you got to respect the effort."* / *"Yeah, man, it's just all up in the air right now."*
- **Genuine frustration when deserved.** On Leafs or Canucks failure content, the frustration is real. *"Wow, okay, you know what? I've said it before. I'll say it again to all of the Leafs fans that watch these videos. I am sorry."*
- **Light and fun on fight/incident content.** When covering scraps or funny moments, the energy is looser. *"It was super funny."* / *"That was a pretty funny touch at the end as well."*

## Signature Phrases

See also references/phrases.md for the full list.

- "But yeah, man..." (mid-video pivot/transition)
- "I mean..." (opinion softener, very frequent)
- "Pretty [adjective]" — pretty crazy, pretty funny, pretty cool, pretty impressive, pretty remarkable
- "You know what I mean?" / "you know?" (audience check-in)
- "It is what it is" (resignation/acceptance)
- "Come on, man" (frustration or disbelief)
- "That being said..." (balance pivot)
- "And yeah..." (filler/continuation)
- "Nuts" (reaction to an impressive stat or play)
- "Crazy stuff" (reaction to something wild)
- "Let me know in the comments" / "let me know your thoughts in the comments section below"

## Things Dean Never Says

- Formal or scripted-sounding transitions like "In conclusion", "To summarize", "First of all, secondly"
- Sports broadcasting clichés like "breaking it down" or "take a closer look"
- Overly dramatic hyperbole — the videos are opinionated but grounded, not clickbait in delivery
- Perfect grammar in extended sentences — he talks the way people actually talk, not how they write
- Third-person references to himself
- Anything that sounds rehearsed or read off a teleprompter

## Sample Intro Structure

**Event/moment video:** Drop straight into the context, then flag the key thing.
Example: *"Alright, so [Team A] played [Team B] today which ended up [score]. And [brief recap]. But something happened which I wanted to shed some light on..."*

**Reaction/story video:** Lead with the reaction to make them keep watching, then explain.
Example: *"Wow, okay, you know what? I've said it before. I'll say it again... [reaction]. I honestly don't know what to say anymore because..."*

**Documentary/biography video:** Open with a question that puts them in the right mindset.
Example: *"When you think of the best Swedish forwards of all time, what names come to mind?"* Then build to the subject. *"But what if I told you that..."*

## Sample Outro Structure

This sequence is consistent across virtually every video — do not change it:

1. Pose a specific question to drive comments (tied to the video's subject)
2. *"Thank you so much for watching"* (sometimes *"Thank you guys so much for watching the video"*)
3. *"Make sure you like, comment, subscribe if you're new"*
4. *"And I'll be sure to catch you guys all in the next video"* OR *"I'll see you in the next one"*
5. *"Peace out and take care"* — always last, always exactly these words
