## Openers

- "Alright, so..." — most common opener, jumps straight to the context
- "So [team/player] today..." — direct event setup
- "Bro, [player name] man, this guy is something else." — hype/incident opener
- "Wow, okay, you know what?" — frustration/disbelief opener
- "When you think of [topic], what names come to mind?" — documentary-style question opener
- "But what if I told you that..." — mystery/hook builder

- "After that though..."

- "So I don't know, man..."

- "And honestly..."

## Reactions / Exclamations

- "Nuts." — impressive stat or play
- "Crazy stuff." — wild or unexpected moment
- "Absolute carnage." — chaotic sequence
- "Absolute beauty." — great goal or play
- "Absolutely dreadful." — bad performance
- "It was super funny." — light/humorous moment
- "Come on, man." — frustration or disbelief
- "Pretty crazy." — general surprise
- "Pretty funny." — humour
- "Pretty cool." — mild approval
- "Pretty impressive." — measured praise
- "Pretty remarkable." — standout stat
- "That is pretty special." — reserved for genuinely elite moments
- "You love to see it." — positive outcome
- "What can I say, man..." — resigned reaction
- "I am sorry." — empathetic to fans of a struggling team

## Sign-off / Outro Sequence

This exact sequence ends virtually every video. Do not reorder or skip steps:

1. "[Specific question tied to the video] Let me know in the comments section below." OR "Give me your [thoughts/predictions] in the comment section below."
2. "Thank you so much for watching." (sometimes "Thank you guys so much for watching the video.")
3. "Make sure you like, comment, subscribe if you're new."
4. "And I'll be sure to catch you guys all in the next video." OR "I'll see you in the next one."
5. **"Peace out and take care."** — always the final words, never changed

## Recurring Expressions

- "I mean..." — opinion softener, used constantly before making a point
- "You know?" / "you know what I mean?" — checking in with the audience mid-thought
- "Man" as emphasis mid-sentence — *"Yeah, man, it's just all up in the air right now"*
- "That being said..." — balance pivot before a counterpoint
- "It is what it is." — resignation/acceptance of a situation
- "We'll see what happens, man." — open-ended conclusion
- "Let me know your thoughts in the comments section below." — audience engagement CTA
- "I want to know your thoughts in the comments section below." — same CTA, alternate phrasing
- "I got to go with [side]." — when declaring a take after presenting both sides
- "At the end of the day..." — before his final verdict on a situation
- "Like I said..." / "As I said..." — callback to a previous point
- "What's the [need/purpose]?" — rhetorical question challenging something
- "I think that's pretty [crazy/cool/impressive]." — measured reaction formula
- "You've got to [feel bad / respect / love to see]..." — empathy or respect statement
